about

Hi, everyone! I'm Yurchik, a trainee frontend developer. I’ve already coded my first website on a commercial basis (maybe I’ll add it to the "Projects" section as a reminder of "How not to do it!"). Despite its imperfections, this project gave me valuable experience, practical knowledge, and even earned me about $100. For many, such a “success” might mark the end of their IT journey, but not for me.

Learning IT is a strange thing. When I moved my first HTML element on a webpage, I thought I was the God of frontend development. But as I delved deeper, and I realized how little I actually knew.

Nowadays, to get a job as a junior frontend developer, you need to master a whole arsenal of tools and technologies: HTML, CSS, SASS, GULP, WebStorm, JavaScript, TypeScript, React, Redux, Git, Next.js, Node.js, Tailwind, MongoDB... And on top of that, it feels like you’re expected to know:

How to manage a spaceship? How to reverse global warming? How much milk is needed for the perfect cappuccino? And the exact difference between position: absolute; and position: fixed; Are you kidding me?! And let’s not forget about artificial intelligence—it writes code faster than I do!

I may not be an engineering genius, but I bring a unique combination of knowledge, experience, and skills to the table. I’m determined to create valuable products for people and secure my first job offer in the IT industry.
